# Competitive Positioning Matrix

Date: March 9, 2026
Audience: Internal only
Purpose: Define the realistic competitive battlefield for Valdrics based on current public market positioning.

## Market Anchor

The FinOps Foundation's current framework and 2025 updates continue to push FinOps beyond pure public cloud into broader Cloud+ operating scope. That makes a cloud-plus-software governance position strategically valid, but it does not erase the advantage established vendors have in proof, breadth, and procurement trust.

Sources:
- FinOps Framework: https://www.finops.org/framework/
- FinOps Capabilities: https://www.finops.org/framework/capabilities/
- Framework 2025 overview: https://www.finops.org/insights/2025-finops-framework/

## Where Valdrics Can Compete Well

1. Cloud + SaaS + software governance in one operating model.
2. Owner-routed action instead of alert-only visibility.
3. Governed remediation and reviewable decision trails.
4. Self-serve-to-mid-market buying motion without forcing every serious buyer into enterprise procurement.

## Where Valdrics Is Not Yet Strong Enough

1. Public customer proof and referenceability.
2. Procurement familiarity and market trust.
3. Connector-breadth claims versus mature incumbents.
4. Mature benchmark authority and unit-cost brand association.

## Strategic Rule

Valdrics should compete as a focused challenger, not as an all-things-to-all-buyers category incumbent.

That means:
1. Lead with governed control, not generic cost visibility.
2. Lead with ownership and action, not connector count.
3. Lead with one shared finance/engineering/security review loop, not a giant feature catalog.
4. Use enterprise language only where procurement, private deployment, or identity lifecycle requirements actually justify it.
