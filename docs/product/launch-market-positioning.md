# Launch Market Positioning

- Date: 2026-03-21
- Status: Active
- Owners: Product, Marketing, Founder

## Public Posture

Valdrics currently uses a `US + UK first` public commercial posture.

Africa remains a supported evaluation market, but the public site does not
default its buying language, ROI displays, or public pricing posture to a
Nigeria-first assumption.

Broader regional expansion is handled through enterprise-led diligence rather
than a generic one-page global claim.

## Why

- The public landing page must optimize for a specific buying motion instead of
  trying to feel equally native to every region at once.
- US and UK buyers are the current primary commercial posture for self-serve and
  early enterprise evaluation.
- Africa remains strategically important, but public defaults should not assume
  a single currency or a single country-wide buying model.
- Region-specific deployment, data-residency, and procurement requirements are
  better handled through the enterprise review path than implied in generic
  marketing copy.

## Public Site Implications

- Public ROI and planning surfaces default to the detected local currency and
  always expose an explicit toggle back to `USD`.
- Public pricing remains listed in `USD`.
- The landing page and public trust surfaces emphasize:
  - governed action
  - proof and reviewability
  - deployment/residency review through the enterprise path
- The site should expose a real company/founder surface and avoid fabricated
  customer proof.

## Notes

- This document records the current public-market posture.
- It supersedes silent assumptions in older billing and market-fit material
  without invalidating the billing-processor decision itself.
